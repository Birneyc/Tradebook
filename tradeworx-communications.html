Customer Communications

Five core messages that cover every stage of the customer journey — from first contact to final payment. Each is written to feel personal, professional, and on-brand.

Every message uses the customer's first name and includes a booking reference. Never send a message that a customer could read and think "this is automated" — even when it is.

01 — Welcome
Message 01
Welcome to Tradeworx
Sent when a customer is first registered on the platform — before any booking is made. Sets the tone and tells them what to expect.
✉ Email
From: Tradeworx <hello@tradeworx.works>
To: Sarah Clarke <sarah@example.com>
You're now with Tradeworx

Hi Sarah,

Welcome — we're really glad you're here.

We're Tradeworx, and we handle the scheduling, communications, and payment side of your service visits so you only have to deal with the work itself. From the moment a booking is made to the receipt landing in your inbox, we'll keep you informed every step of the way.

Here's what you can expect from us:

Booking confirmed — you'll hear from us as soon as your appointment is set, with your engineer's name, arrival time, and a reference number.

Day-of reminder — a quick message on the morning of your visit so nothing slips through.

Any changes — if anything shifts, you'll know straight away — no surprises.

You can always reply to any message we send and someone will get back to you. We don't do automated replies that go nowhere.

Looking forward to working with you.

The Tradeworx Team
hello@tradeworx.works · tradeworx.works
You're receiving this because your details were registered on the Tradeworx platform. Reply to unsubscribe at any time.
When to send: On first registration, before any booking exists. Can also be used when an existing customer is added to a new business on the platform.
Tone note: Warm and informative — not a legal disclaimer. The goal is to make the customer feel looked after before anything has even happened.

03 — Delayed Appointment
Message 03
Appointment Running Late
Sent as soon as a delay is reported — by admin or the engineer directly. Acknowledges the inconvenience without over-apologising.
💬 SMS ● WhatsApp
TW
Tradeworx
tradeworx.works
Hi Sarah, we're really sorry — your 10:00am appointment is running approximately 30 minutes late due to a previous job overrunning. James is now expected to arrive around 10:30am. We appreciate your patience. If you need to reach us in the meantime, please reply to this message — Tradeworx
Delivered · 09:47
{first_name} {original_time} {delay_duration} {delay_reason} {new_eta} {engineer_name}
When to send: Immediately when a delay is reported in Tradeworx. The message preview is auto-generated from the delay duration and reason selected — the engineer or admin sends it in one tap.
Tone note: Lead with the apology, but keep it brief. Give the new ETA immediately — that's all the customer actually needs. Don't over-explain or list multiple reasons.

Using these templates in Tradeworx

Messages 02, 03, and 04 are generated automatically by the platform using the booking data — the variables in {curly braces} are filled in at send time. Message 05 fires via the Stripe webhook when payment is confirmed. Message 01 can be customised per-business in a future release. All messages respect the Default Channel setting in the Communications view — switch between SMS, WhatsApp, or both without changing the message content.
